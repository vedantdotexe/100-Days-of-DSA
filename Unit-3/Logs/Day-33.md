### 🟢 Day 33: April 11, 2026
**Topic:** Merge Sort (Divide & Conquer)
**Language:** Java ☕
**Vibe:** 🧩 Putting the Pieces Together

Today I moved from simple $O(n^2)$ sorts to Merge Sort, which runs in $O(n \log n)$. This was my first real experience with recursion in sorting. The "Merge" step is the most important part—it's where the actual sorting happens as the smaller pieces are stitched back together. It uses more memory (extra arrays), but the speed difference is massive.
